PASTRIES

Pastry refers to a variety of doughs, as well as the sweet and savoury baked goods made from them. The dough may be accordingly called pastry dough for clarity. Sweetened pastries are often described as baker's confectionery. And can be used in any type of event;these include puffpuff,scotch egg,chinchin and so on....

Meat Pie

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 250g (1 cup) margarine or butter (cold, cut into cubes)
  • 1/2 cup cold water (or as needed)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

For the Filling:

  • 300g minced meat (beef or your choice)
  • 2 medium potatoes (peeled and diced small)
  • 1 medium carrot (peeled and diced)
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 1 seasoning cube (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp flour (for thickening)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup water or stock

Preparation:

  1. Make the Filling: Heat oil in a pan, add onions, and cook until soft. Add minced meat and cook until browned.
  2. Add diced carrots and potatoes. Cook for 3–5 minutes, then add seasoning, salt, pepper, and a little water or stock. Simmer until vegetables are soft.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of flour to thicken the mixture. Stir well and set aside to cool completely.
  4. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, mix flour, baking powder, and salt. Add cold margarine and rub into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  5. Add cold water a little at a time until it forms a smooth dough. Do not overwork the dough. Cover and let rest for 10 minutes.
  6. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  7. Shape the Pies: Roll out the dough on a floured surface. Cut into circles (using a bowl or cutter).
  8. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle. Fold over and seal the edges with a fork.
  9. Place on the baking tray. Beat 1 egg and brush the tops for a golden finish.
  10. Bake for 25–30 minutes or until golden brown. Let cool slightly before serving.

Fish Roll

Ingredient

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup margarine or butter (softened)
  • 1/4 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1 egg (for the dough)
  • 6 boiled eggs (peeled)
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying

Preparation:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt together.
  2. Add margarine and mix with fingers until it looks like breadcrumbs.
  3. Add one egg and water gradually. Mix until it forms a smooth but firm dough.
  4. Divide the dough into portions, depending on the number of boiled eggs.
  5. Flatten each dough portion and wrap it around a boiled egg, sealing all edges.
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  7. Fry the wrapped eggs until golden brown and cooked through, turning occasionally.
  8. Drain on paper towels and allow to cool slightly before serving.

Chinchin

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 cup margarine or butter (softened)
  • 1/4 cup milk (or water)
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg (optional for flavor)
  • Vegetable oil (for deep frying)

Preparation:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, salt, baking powder, and nutmeg (if using).
  2. Add the margarine and mix until crumbly.
  3. Add the egg and milk. Mix to form a smooth but firm dough.
  4. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/4 inch thickness.
  5. Cut into small squares or strips using a knife or pizza cutter.
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  7. Fry the chinchin in batches until golden brown and crispy.
  8. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  9. Allow to cool and enjoy!

Puff-Puff

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/4 cups lukewarm water
  • Vegetable oil (for deep frying)
  • Optional: a pinch of nutmeg or cinnamon for extra flavor

Preparation:

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, sugar, salt, and nutmeg (if using).
  2. Dissolve the yeast in lukewarm water and let it sit for 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  3. Combine the yeast mixture with the flour mixture and stir into a smooth, sticky batter.
  4. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  6. Use your hand or a spoon to scoop small portions of the dough into the hot oil.
  7. Fry in batches until golden brown, turning occasionally (about 4–6 minutes per batch).
  8. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  9. Serve warm, optionally dusted with powdered sugar.

Coconut chin-chin

Ingredients

  • 2 cups grated fresh coconut (or desiccated coconut)
  • 1 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional: a few drops of food coloring (e.g. pink or green)
  • Butter or oil for greasing tray

Preparation:

  1. Grease a flat tray or plate and set aside.
  2. In a non-stick saucepan, combine the coconut, condensed milk, and sugar.
  3. Place over medium heat and stir continuously for about 10–15 minutes, until the mixture thickens and pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  4. Add vanilla extract and food coloring (if using), and stir well.
  5. Pour the hot mixture into the greased tray and spread evenly.
  6. Allow it to cool slightly, then cut into squares while still warm.
  7. Let it cool completely before serving or storing in an airtight container.

Buns

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ cups (440g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm milk
  • ¼ cup (50g) sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 large egg
  • ⅓ cup (75g) unsalted butter, softened
  • Extra flour for dusting

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm milk and let it sit for 5–10 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt.
  3. Add the yeast mixture, egg, and softened butter to the dry ingredients.
  4. Knead the dough for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  6. Punch down the dough and divide it into 12 equal portions.
  7. Shape each portion into a bun and place on a baking tray lined with parchment paper.
  8. Cover and let the buns rise for another 30–40 minutes.
  9. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake buns for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
  10. Cool on a wire rack before serving.

Sausage Roll

Ingredients

  • 500g sausage meat
  • 1 sheet of puff pastry (ready-rolled)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried herbs (thyme or sage)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Prepartion:

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
  2. In a bowl, mix the sausage meat, onion, garlic, herbs, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roll out the puff pastry and cut it in half lengthwise.
  4. Place the sausage meat mixture along one edge of each pastry strip.
  5. Roll the pastry over the filling and seal the edge with a bit of egg wash.
  6. Cut into smaller rolls and place on a baking tray.
  7. Brush the tops with egg wash for a golden finish.
  8. Bake for 20–25 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through.

Coconut Chin-Chin

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup grated or desiccated coconut
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk (or water)
  • 1/4 cup butter (softened)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying

Preparation:

  1. In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, grated coconut, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add the softened butter and mix until crumbly.
  3. Gradually add coconut milk and vanilla (if using) to form a soft but non-sticky dough.
  4. On a floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/4 inch thickness.
  5. Cut into small cubes or strips using a knife or cutter.
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  7. Fry the chin chin in batches until golden brown and crisp, stirring occasionally for even cooking.
  8. Remove and drain on paper towels. Let cool completely before storing in an airtight container.

Doughnut

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup warm water (about 110°F or 45°C)
  • 3/4 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Sugar or glaze for topping (optional)

Preparation:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm water and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine milk, sugar, salt, egg, melted butter, and the yeast mixture.
  3. Gradually add flour, mixing until a dough forms.Knead on a floured surface for about 5 minutes until smooth.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.
  5. Roll out the dough to about 1/2 inch thickness. Cut with a doughnut cutter or round cookie cutters.
  6. Let cut doughnuts rise again for 30-45 minutes until puffy.
  7. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C).
  8. Fry doughnuts for 1-2 minutes per side until golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  9. Dust with sugar or dip in glaze while warm, if desired.

Scotch Egg

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 450g (1 lb) sausage meat
  • 1 teaspoon mustard (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 100g (3.5 oz) plain flour
  • 2 additional eggs, beaten
  • 150g (5 oz) breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying

Preparation:

  1. Boil 4 eggs for 7 minutes, then transfer to cold water, peel and set aside.
  2. In a bowl, mix sausage meat with mustard, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  3. Divide sausage mix into 4 portions. Flatten each and wrap around each egg completely.
  4. Roll each sausage-covered egg in flour, then dip in beaten egg, and coat with breadcrumbs.
  5. Heat oil in a deep fryer or deep pan to 170°C (340°F).
  6. Fry each Scotch egg for 5–6 minutes or until golden brown and fully cooked.
  7. Drain on paper towels. Serve warm or cold.

Hot Dog

Ingredients

  • 4 hot dog sausages (beef or chicken)
  • 4 soft bread rolls (Agege bread or hot dog buns)
  • 2 tablespoons suya spice (yaji)
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo), chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 small tomato, chopped
  • Mayonnaise and ketchup (optional)
  • Sliced cabbage or lettuce (optional)

Preparation:

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add chopped tomato, onion, and scotch bonnet pepper. Fry for 5–7 minutes until soft and fragrant.
  2. Add the sausages to the pan and fry for 3–5 minutes until browned. Sprinkle suya spice over them and cook for another 1–2 minutes.
  3. Slice the bread rolls open (not completely through). Lightly toast if desired.
  4. Place a sausage into each roll. Top with the fried pepper-onion sauce.
  5. Optionally, add mayonnaise, ketchup, or fresh cabbage for extra crunch.
